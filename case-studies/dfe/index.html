Teachers Student Loan Reimbursement

Department for Education is a ministerial department, responsible for children’s services and education, including early years, schools, higher and further education policy, apprenticeships and wider skills in England.

They needed to build a service that delivered a policy commitment to reimburses teacher’s student loans for secondary teachers who taught languages, science and computing.

Alpha

DfE uses the Government Digital Service model for phases of an agile project.

A team was formed to deliver an Alpha phase in 12 weeks. The goals was to prototype the first version of the end-to-end service.

I divided my time between service design and user research.

User Research

Working with a junior user researcher, over 7 sprints, we recruited participants and ran research to build evidence for the service design. We spoke to 111 eligible users and mapped all the insights back to every step in the service journey. We used different methods including phone interviews, face-to-face, lab sessions and pop-up interviews at a teaching event.

Orchestrating the layers of the service

I collaborated with:

  • the service owner (a senior civil servant), a product manager and interaction designer to create a working prototype of the digital service
  • a technical architect and other ministerial departments (for example HMRC) to bring together supporting systems that would be needed. Eg. GOV.UK identity tool called Verify

Outcome

We delivered:

  • a digital prototype of the end-to-end service with a working technology stack
  • a hypothesis board of evidence relating to all the steps in the service design
  • a service blueprint that detailed every step in the service in relation to user needs, actors, systems and policy criteria
  • my team passed the service assessment and the service went into the Beta phase
